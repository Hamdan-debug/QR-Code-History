The History of QR Codes

Introduction Origin Evolution Applications

Introduction

QR codes, or Quick Response codes, are two-dimensional barcodes that can be scanned using a smartphone or dedicated QR scanner. They have become a common sight in various industries due to their ability to store a significant amount of information.

Origin

The QR code was invented in 1994 by Masahiro Hara, an engineer at Denso Wave, a subsidiary of Toyota.
It was initially designed to track vehicle parts during manufacturing processes.

Evolution

Over the years, QR codes evolved beyond industrial use. With the rise of smartphones, they became widely adopted for marketing, payments, and information sharing.
The ability to encode URLs made them an essential tool for bridging physical and digital worlds.

Applications

QR codes are now used in various fields, including:

  • Mobile payments
  • Event ticketing
  • Product labeling
  • Contactless information sharing
  • Quizzes and Wi-fi access
